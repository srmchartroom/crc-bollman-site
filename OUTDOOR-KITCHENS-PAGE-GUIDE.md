# Outdoor Kitchens Page Guide

## Research Notes

Outdoor kitchens are one of the clearest "I'm not price shopping" signals. This page should feel aspirational and expensive (because it is). Anyone typing "custom outdoor kitchen" is not calling three guys with pickup trucks. This is lifestyle-driven, high-margin work.

---

## Target Keywords

**Primary:** "outdoor kitchen builders"

**Secondary:**
- "custom outdoor kitchens"
- "outdoor kitchen designers"
- "outdoor kitchen contractors"
- "outdoor kitchen installation"

---

## Service Details from Client

- **Project cost range:** $2,000 to $100,000 depending on size and features
- Outdoor kitchen calls almost always expand into patios, lighting, drainage, and more
- **Partners:** Barbecue Guys (certified builder), Brio (fire features AND outdoor furniture — clients often don't know Brio does furniture), Solo (fire pits)
- **Highest-end fire feature:** Outdoor fireplaces with full chimney, wood storage boxes, and optional integrated brick pizza oven

### Two Approaches to Outdoor Kitchens

1. **Fully Custom** — Bollman builds cabinets, applies stone individually, installs all appliances
2. **Barbecue Guys Kit System** — Pre-paneled stone, faster labor, identical finished appearance; clients cannot tell the difference

Bollman became a Barbecue Guys certified builder specifically to serve clients who wanted outdoor kitchens at a more accessible price point.

**Example: Kaiser Project** (referred by realtor Emily) — Veneered existing stamped concrete patio, expanded with pavers, Barbecue Guys kit with two islands (one for client's existing pizza oven), full appliances — total $15,000. Fully custom equivalent would have been ~$32,000.

---

## Page Structure

Should answer: What does this look like, what's possible, and why should Bollman build it? But not in a "here's what an outdoor kitchen is" way. In an **aspire --> inform --> prove --> transform --> convert** way.

The emotional arc matters because clients are not only making a rational purchase, they're making an emotional one; messaging should speak to both. The strongest messaging should move in this order:

**The Client Dream** (transformation + family-life payoff)
--> **The Bollman Difference** (honest + caring + expert approach)
--> --> **The Technical Proof** (credentials + testimonials + more)

---

### Section 1: THE HERO (Hook)

The opening visual and headline. Set the tone immediately: this is premium, this is lifestyle, this is real. Full-bleed imagery of a finished outdoor kitchen that feels like it belongs on a magazine spread. The hero should make the visitor feel like they've arrived at the right place — not a contractor page, a design-build page.

---

### Section 2: THE DREAM (What Are They Seeking?)

**Purpose:** Speak to the emotional and psychological reason the ICP is investing.

This is where you paint the picture of the transformation — the family dinner outside, the pride of hosting, the feeling that the backyard finally matches the rest of the home. Not features, not specs. The dream. The aspiration. The end result they can already see in their head.

What this section should communicate:
- The outdoor kitchen is not just an appliance — it's a way of living
- This is about memories, pride, entertaining, and daily quality of life
- The backyard becomes the best room in the house

---

### Section 3: THE DETAILS (What Do They Need to Know to Buy?)

**Purpose:** Service info — about the service, the materials, the process.

This is where rational confidence meets emotional desire. Cover what Bollman actually does:

- **Two build approaches:** Fully custom vs. Barbecue Guys kit system (and why both deliver the same finished result)
- **Scope of work:** Cabinets, stone, appliances, islands, grills, pizza ovens, fire features, lighting, drainage, patio integration
- **Materials and partners:** Barbecue Guys (certified builder), Brio (fire features + outdoor furniture), Solo (fire pits)
- **Price anchoring:** Projects range from $2K to $100K+ — and outdoor kitchen calls almost always expand into patios, lighting, drainage, and more
- **The process:** What it looks like to work with Bollman from first conversation to finished space
- **High-end options:** Full outdoor fireplaces with chimney, wood storage boxes, and optional integrated brick pizza oven

Use the Kaiser project story to illustrate the kit approach: $15K vs. $32K for identical appearance. This is a trust-building, objection-killing detail.

---

### Section 4: THE PROOF (Why Should They Trust You?)

**Purpose:** Proof section — relevant industry partnerships, features, testimonials.

- Certified Barbecue Guys builder (and why that matters)
- Brio partnership (fire features + furniture — clients often don't realize Brio does furniture)
- Solo partnership
- Relevant project testimonials
- Any industry recognition, features, or press
- Before/after or project gallery if available

---

### Section 5: THE ANCHOR (Anchoring Statement)

**Purpose:** Transformation-based, emotional, tying back to the dream-state and end-goal desire.

A single powerful statement or short passage that reconnects the reader to the vision from Section 2. This isn't a CTA — it's a pause. A moment to let the aspiration land again, now backed by the proof and details they just absorbed. Think of it as the emotional bridge between "I trust them" and "I'm ready to call."

---

### Section 6: THE FACTS (FAQs)

**Purpose:** Address remaining rational objections and practical questions.

Likely questions:
- How much does an outdoor kitchen cost?
- How long does it take to build?
- What's the difference between custom and kit-built?
- Do you handle permits?
- Can you add an outdoor kitchen to an existing patio?
- What brands/appliances do you work with?
- Do outdoor kitchens need plumbing or gas lines?
- What maintenance is required?

---

### Section 7: CONVERT (Final CTA)

**Purpose:** Revisit the transformation in 1-2 sentences, then move into the close.

This is not a hard sell. It's a warm, confident invitation. Reconnect the dream to the action: you've seen what's possible, you've seen the proof, now let's talk about your space. One clear CTA — schedule a consultation.

## Brand Voice + Messaging + Tone

Bollman's voice should sound like the business itself: experienced, honest, warm, and deeply capable. It should communicate premium quality without pretension, technical expertise without dryness, and family-rooted care without becoming overly sentimental. Bollman should never sound like a generic contractor, a luxury cliche, or a corporate brand trying too hard to impress.

**At its best, Bollman's voice should feel like:** A trusted expert with a warm presence, a sharp eye, and the confidence to tell you the truth.

### Core Messaging Themes

Across the website and broader brand content, the messaging should consistently communicate:
- Outdoor spaces that feel like a true extension of the home
- Beauty and long-term performance, not one or the other
- Honest guidance and expert execution
- Family-rooted care backed by real experience
- Premium design-build without guesswork
- Confidence that the work is being done right

### The 4 Tone Pillars

1. **Clear + Confident:** Bollman should speak with the confidence of real expertise.
2. **Warm + Human:** Even at its most premium, the brand should still feel approachable.
3. **Honest + Straight-Talking:** A strong advantage — this is part of what makes them feel trustworthy.
4. **Elevated + Rooted:** Bollman should sound premium, but never pretentious.
